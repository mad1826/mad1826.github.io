Protein

A protein is a large molecule made up of amino acids ( source: Brittanica ). They may be composed of about 20 different amino acids, and each animal species and their organs may process different types of proteins. This variety means that the purpose of one protein may be different from another's. However, most proteins still share similar qualities. The Australian government site Better Health Channel found the following general purposes:

  • Helps form and support muscles and bones
  • Produces necessary enzymes and hormones
  • Provides the body with an energy source

Proteins are a common property in foods. They are not tied to any specific food group. Rather, modern nutritional guides such as MyPlate may categorize protein as a group on its own. Nevertheless, the most common sources of protein are found in meats and dairy products. Healthline found the following amounts of protein are in one serving of each respective food:

Food Protein per serving (g)
Eggs 6.3
Almonds 6.0
Chicken Breast (half) 26.7
